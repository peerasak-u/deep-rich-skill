1. Executive Summary

Snowflake

The signature view is a visual score across five criteria: valuation, future growth, past performance, financial health, and dividend.

Narrative

The page turns raw financial data into strengths, weaknesses, risks, and a readable investor story.

Forecasts

Future growth, fair value, analyst estimates, and intrinsic value are not pure facts. Treat them as sourced opinions/models.

Adaptation

We can safely copy the information architecture, not the proprietary scoring, visuals, branding, or forecast claims.

Main answer

A Simply Wall St report is not just a company profile. It is a structured stock-analysis dashboard: overview, risk checks, valuation, future growth, past performance, financial health, dividends, management, ownership, and data freshness.

Important boundary

For our own report, keep factual company profile sections strong, but label or omit forecast-heavy sections unless we have licensed/sourced analyst estimates and a transparent model.

4. What We Can Safely Provide vs What to Avoid

Safe factual sections

Business description and operating segments
Exchange, ticker, market, industry, fiscal year-end
Historical revenue, earnings, margins, assets, liabilities, cash, debt
Share price history and volatility if sourced
Dividend history and declared buybacks
Management, board, ownership, insider filings
Explicit data freshness table

Use carefully or omit

Intrinsic/fair value if model is not disclosed
“Undervalued/overvalued” conclusions without methodology
Future revenue, earnings, EPS, or ROE forecasts without licensed analyst data
Dividend sustainability predictions without assumptions
Proprietary snowflake scoring or copied UI language
Buy/sell pressure or personalized advice

6. Detailed Engineering Spec for Coding Agents

Purpose: Use this to build our own Simply Wall St-inspired company profile report without copying proprietary scoring or making unsourced forecasts.

Goal and non-goals

TypeDescription
GoalCreate a single-company HTML report that feels like a modern stock dashboard and separates facts, calculations, estimates, and interpretation.
Non-goalDo not reproduce Simply Wall St’s snowflake UI, proprietary checks, exact copy, or forecast conclusions without licensed data.

Recommended report structure

1. Hero: company name, ticker, exchange, industry, last updated
2. Business profile: what the company does, segments, geography, revenue model
3. Key facts: market cap, share price date, fiscal year, reporting currency
4. Risk flags: explicit rule + source + severity
5. Historical financials: revenue, profit, margins, cash flow, balance sheet
6. Valuation facts: P/E, P/S, EV/EBITDA, peer table; avoid fair value unless model disclosed
7. Dividends and buybacks: history, yield, payout ratio if calculable
8. Management and ownership: CEO, board, insider/institutional ownership
9. Strengths / weaknesses / questions: narrative summary
10. Data status: source, period, retrieval date, stale-data warnings

Acceptance criteria

Every forward-looking statement is labeled Estimate and has source/date.
Every calculated metric shows formula or source inputs.
Report includes a data freshness table.
Risk flags use visible rules, not hidden model claims.
No buy/sell recommendation language unless user explicitly asks and guardrails are followed.
